Stellar Build Camp — From Idea to Impact

Organised by GDG on Campus, IIT Mandi

When I first took on the role of GDG on Campus Organizer at IIT Mandi, I wanted our community to experience more than just talks and workshops. I wanted them to build, experiment, and ship real products.

That vision led to the creation of Stellar Build Camp — a hands-on builder program designed to introduce students to the world of Web3, blockchain development, and open innovation.

The Goal

To help beginners cross the intimidating gap between learning and building.

What We Built

Stellar Build Camp was structured as a multi-day immersive program that combined:

  • Fundamentals of blockchain and Web3
  • Practical sessions on smart contracts and decentralised applications
  • Guided project-building sprints
  • Mentorship-driven problem solving
  • Community discussions on real-world use-cases beyond hype

Instead of passive lectures, participants were encouraged to explore ideas, form mini-teams, and prototype solutions that mattered to them.

My Role as Organizer

As the GDG Organizer, I led the initiative end-to-end:

  • Conceptualising the program structure
  • Designing the learning roadmap
  • Coordinating speakers and mentors
  • Managing registrations and participant onboarding
  • Ensuring a beginner-friendly, inclusive environment

What stood out the most was watching students who had never written a smart contract before deploy their first on-chain logic by the end of the camp.

"This experience reshaped my understanding of community leadership. It is not about scale or numbers — it is about creating spaces where people feel confident enough to try, fail, and build again. Stellar Build Camp was not just an event. It was a starting point for many builders at IIT Mandi."

India Blockchain Week — Seeing the Ecosystem Up Close

Bengaluru, India

Attending India Blockchain Week (IBW) was a turning point in how I view the Web3 ecosystem.

Until then, my exposure was limited to online courses, communities, and documentation. IBW placed me right inside the real world of builders, founders, protocol teams, and researchers who are shaping decentralised technology in India.

What I Experienced

IBW was not about buzzwords. It was about:

  • Listening to founders speak about the challenges behind building protocols
  • Understanding how real-world adoption problems differ from textbook solutions
  • Learning how compliance, scalability, UX, and community building intersect in Web3
  • Observing how Indian startups are innovating under constraints rather than copying global playbooks
"The most valuable insight from IBW was this: Web3 is not just a technical movement. It is a systems-level shift that blends technology, policy, user behaviour, and ethics. It made me rethink how I approach learning — moving from feature-driven curiosity to problem-driven building."

GCHI — Learning Inclusion in Technology

Grace Hopper Celebration India

The Grace Hopper Celebration India (GCHI) was not just another tech conference for me. It was deeply personal.

As someone working on NeuroSaarthi — a project focused on neurodiversity awareness and inclusion — GCHI aligned perfectly with my belief that technology should not only advance capability, but also expand access.

What Stood Out

At GCHI, I witnessed:

  • Conversations around accessibility, diversity, and mental health in tech
  • Stories of women navigating systemic barriers with resilience and grace
  • Panels that spoke about leadership beyond job titles — leadership rooted in empathy, impact, and representation
"GCHI reminded me that innovation without inclusion is incomplete. It strengthened my resolve to build technology that acknowledges different learning styles, different abilities, and different lived realities — not as edge cases, but as the core design lens."
